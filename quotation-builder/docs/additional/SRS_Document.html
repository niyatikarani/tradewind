1. System Overview

1.1 Purpose

Web-based quotation management system for S & G Exports, a spice and food products export firm based in Mumbai. Replaces manual spreadsheet estimation with adaptive margins, multi-currency quotations, on-demand price tracking (prices updated when orders come in, not on a fixed daily schedule), container logistics, analytics, Excel-based bulk order upload, feature-level access control, and a centralized business rule engine.

5. Stale Price Blocking New

Hard Rule: Since prices are updated on a need basis (not daily), the system enforces freshness at quote time. If any SKU has a price older than the configurable threshold, quote creation is blocked. This forces the team to call the dealer and get a current price before quoting. Cannot proceed past Step 3 unless overridden by authorized user (quote.stale_override = Super User or Admin).

10. Plain English Explanation

This section explains the entire system in simple, non-technical language — for business owners, marketing team, and anyone who won't be writing code.

What is this?

It's a website (like Gmail or WhatsApp Web) that your team opens in a browser. It replaces the Excel sheets you currently use to calculate quotation prices. Everyone logs in with their own username and password, and the system shows them only the screens relevant to their job.

Why not just keep using Excel?

Excel breaks. Someone edits the wrong cell, a formula stops working, and the price you send to a client is wrong. There's no way to stop someone from accidentally deleting a formula. There's no history of who changed what. Two people can't work on the same file at the same time without conflicts. And adding a new packaging size means editing 5 different sheets and praying nothing breaks.

This system solves all of that. Formulas live in the code, not in cells that anyone can break. Every change is logged. Multiple people work simultaneously. And adding a new product or package size is just filling in a form.

Who uses it?

Think of it like a key card system in an office building. Each person gets a card that opens only the doors they need. The boss (admin) decides which doors each person's card opens, and can change it anytime.

For example:

  • Ravi only enters prices — he sees the price entry screen, the stale price list, and nothing else.
  • Priya only builds quotations — she sees the quote wizard, quote list, and can upload Excel orders.
  • Suresh does both prices AND quotes, plus he can override stale price blocks — he's a "Super User".
  • You (owner) see everything including the settings panel where you control all the business rules.

Tomorrow, if Priya needs to also see price trends, you just tick one checkbox in her profile. Done in 5 seconds. No developer needed.

How does a typical day work?

📞 An order query comes in

Marketing gets an enquiry — maybe a WhatsApp message, maybe a phone call, maybe an email with a handwritten list. Two ways to get it into the system:

  • Option A (Excel upload): You give the marketing guy a standard Excel template. He fills in the product names, quantities, and packaging preferences (or the client fills it). He uploads this file into the system. The system reads it, checks if everything makes sense (does this product exist? is the quantity reasonable?), and flags any problems in green/yellow/red — like a traffic light.
  • Option B (Manual entry): The quotation person types each product one by one into a step-by-step form. The system guides them: pick product → pick quality → pick package size → enter quantity → choose labelling, and so on.

Both options end up at the same place — a list of products ready to be priced.

💰 Getting fresh prices

Here's the important bit: you don't need to update all prices every day. You only update prices when you actually need them for an order. The system knows when a price is "old" (you set the threshold — say 7 days, or 14 days, or whatever makes sense for your business).

When building a quote, if a product's price is too old, the system shows a yellow/red warning right there and gives you an "Update Price" button. You call the dealer, get the current rate, type it in, and continue. No need to leave the quotation screen.

If you try to finish a quote with old prices, the system blocks you — the "Generate Quote" button is literally grayed out. Only a Super User or Admin can override this by clicking a special button and typing a reason (like "dealer confirmed price unchanged"). This prevents anyone from accidentally sending a quote based on prices from 3 weeks ago.

🧮 The system calculates everything

Once all products have fresh prices, the system automatically calculates:

  • Raw material cost — the price you just entered from the dealer
  • Labour cost — based on the package size (smaller packets = more work per kg = more expensive)
  • Packaging cost — cartons, stickers, pouches, or jars
  • Extra costs — sanitization (ETO/Gamma/Steam), certification (FDA, Spice Board, etc.), loading/unloading
  • Transport — getting it to the port

It adds all of these up, converts from Rupees to the client's currency (Dollars, Euros, Pounds, Dirhams — whatever), adds your profit margin, and spreads the container costs (customs, clearing agent) across the order.

📊 Smart profit margin

Instead of always adding a flat 10%, the system suggests a margin based on common sense:

  • Big order? You can afford a smaller margin (volume makes up for it).
  • Client paying 80% advance? Lower risk, so you can offer a better price.
  • Repeat client with good payment history? Reward them with a better rate.
  • Niche product with no competition (like dagad phool)? You can charge more.
  • Prices have been jumping up and down? Add a safety buffer.
  • Rush order in 5 days? Premium pricing for the overtime.
  • Risky country with payment issues? Higher margin to cover the risk.

The system considers all of these and suggests a number. You can always change it — it's a suggestion, not a mandate. But if you set the margin below the minimum (say below 3%), the system flags it and requires the boss to approve.

📦 Container planning

The system calculates how much space (CBM) and weight the order takes, and tells you whether you need a 20-foot or 40-foot container. It shows you how full the container is — if it's 95% full by volume but only 60% by weight, you know you can add more heavy items. This helps when negotiating with the buyer on order adjustments.

📄 Sending the quote

Pick how the client wants to see prices — per packet, per carton, per pound, per tonne, or all of them. Click a button. Out comes a professional PDF, or an Excel file, or a CSV. The quote has S & G Exports branding, the rate used for currency conversion, and your terms & conditions at the bottom.

What if I need to change a number manually?

Any calculated number in the system can be clicked and changed by hand. Say the system calculated labour at ₹25/kg but you know it's actually ₹30 because of a special requirement — just click the number, type 30, give a reason, done. The system marks it with an orange border so everyone knows it was manually changed. There's always a "reset" button to go back to the calculated value. Only people with permission can do this.

What if a client comes back for a revised quote?

One-click duplicate. The system copies the entire quote, updates all prices to today's rates, and highlights what changed. So if turmeric went from ₹120 to ₹135 since the last quote, you see it immediately in yellow. Adjust and send.

What about the settings?

Everything that might change — the 3% minimum margin, the 7-day stale threshold, which sanitization is standard for USA shipments, the container weight limits, the clearing agent cost, the currency exchange buffer — all of it lives in one place called the Rule Engine. It's like a settings panel with 13 tabs. Only the admin (you) can access it.

The clever part: you can set rules at different levels. A global rule says "minimum margin is 3%." But for one important client, you can set their minimum to 1%. For USA shipments, you can set "always use ETO sanitization." For Basmati Rice to EU specifically, you can override that to "use Gamma instead." The system automatically knows which rule to apply — the most specific one always wins.

Is my data safe?

Every night, the system automatically copies the entire database to your company's OneDrive (the Microsoft 365 you're already paying for). It keeps the last 5 days. If something goes wrong, recovery takes 30 seconds — just restore one file.

Every single change — who entered a price, who created a quote, who overrode a margin, who changed a setting — is logged with the person's name and the exact time. Nothing is ever truly deleted. You can always trace back what happened.

What does it cost to run?

The software itself is free — built with free tools. Hosting is either free (basic plan on Railway or Render) or ₹500/month if you want a private server. Currency exchange rates come from a free API. The OneDrive backup uses your existing Microsoft 365 subscription. There are no per-user fees, no monthly software subscriptions.

How long to build?

36 working days for one developer. That's roughly 7–8 weeks. The price entry system works first (by week 2), quotations start working around week 4, and the full system with analytics and the settings panel is done by week 8.
